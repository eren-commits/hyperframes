the page is sunlight. the type is unhurried. the colours are seasons, not signals.

i — Palette

Paper, ink,
and three seasons.

Warm paper canvas. Soft black ink. Three colour accents — sage, blush, and lemon — each used as the entire mood of a slide, never together in one composition.

ii — Typography

Cormorant,
set generously.

Cormorant Garamond carries both display and body. Italics for emphasis, roman for accent words. Inter sans is reserved for the smallest UI, JetBrains Mono for chrome labels.

iii — Surface

Paper, then
more paper.

Surfaces are nearly invisible — a hairline border, perhaps, but no shadows. The page is the surface; the canvas is the page.

iv — Motion

Slow.
Considered.

Motion is patient. Long durations, gentle eases. The page should feel turned, not flicked.

v — Guidelines

Sic et
non.

The rules are few. The page softens the moment any are violated.

sic.

  • Choose one season per slide: sage, blush, or lemon.
  • Set every headline in italic Cormorant.
  • Keep page margins generous — 156px, never less.
  • Use mono only for chrome labels.
  • Tint neutrals warm; never cool grey.

non.

  • Combine all three accents in one composition.
  • Use sans-serif for body — Cormorant carries the page.
  • Apply shadows or gradients. Surfaces stay flat.
  • Use uppercase for body. Italic is emphasis.
  • Crowd the margin. Density is the enemy of attention.
