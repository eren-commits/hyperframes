— Manifesto

type holds silence. the page is the argument. the reader is a slow walker, and the deck is a long sentence.

ii — Typography

Cormorant,
almost always italic.

Cormorant Garamond does nearly all the speaking — italics for emphasis, roman for accent words. Inter holds the sans column for small chrome, JetBrains Mono runs labels.

iii — Surface

Paper, then
ink.

Surfaces are nearly invisible. Hairline borders, no shadows. The page is the surface; the canvas is the page.

A surface is a marginal thing. A border, perhaps; a numeral, perhaps; otherwise the words alone must hold the page together. The accent is rationed: precisely one gilded element per surface.

iv — Motion

Slow.
Considered.

Motion is patient. Long durations, gentle eases. The page should feel turned, not flicked.

// primary easing

quintic.out

Long tail, soft landing. The page resolves rather than arrives. Use for major entrances — chapter headings, datum reveals, treatise margins.

cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1)
// duration

1.2s

Slides settle in 1.2s. Entrances 0.8s. Hovers 0.4s. The reader is unhurried; so is the deck.

v — Guidelines

Sic et
non.

The rules are short. The system collapses the moment any are violated.

sic.

  • Use gold for one element per page — the thing to be remembered.
  • Set headlines in italic Cormorant. Roman is reserved for inline emphasis.
  • Keep margins generous — 144px page padding, never less.
  • Use small caps mono for chrome — folio numbers, marginalia, labels.
  • Tint everything toward the gold; nothing toward neutral grey.

non.

  • Introduce a second accent. Gold is the only voice.
  • Use sans-serif for body — it strips the page of its voice.
  • Apply shadows. The page is flat; weight comes from ink alone.
  • Use uppercase for body. Italic carries the emphasis.
  • Crowd the margin. Density is the enemy of attention.
